About

This project is a capstone project of the CUSP NYU master program sponsored by TransitCenter, aiming to build a performance dashboard that communicates overall reliability metrics to the public, on a line-by-line basis.

We hope this project might:

  1. Assist transit agencies and advocates to take advantage of the latest data and analytical tools to optimize planning, service delivery, and public engagement.
  2. Raise the expectations for transit in NYC while providing a practical vision and popular support for achieving them.

This project is still in the beta version, so if there is any bug, miscalculation, please contact us below.

NYU | Center for Urban Science and Progress

New York University's Center for Urban Science & Progress (CUSP) is a university-wide center whose research and education programs are focused on urban informatics. Using NYC as its lab, and building from its home in the NYU Tandon School of Engineering, it integrates and applies NYU strengths in the natural, data, and social sciences to understand and improve cities throughout the world. CUSP offers a one-year MS degree in Applied Urban Science & Informatics.
