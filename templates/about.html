About

This project is a capstone project of the CUSP master program sponsered by TransitCenter.

The proposed project would serve two related objectives we have as an organization dedicated to serving the public interest. 2) Assist transit agencies and advocates to take advantage of the latest data and analytical tools to optimize planning, service delivery, and public engagement. 2) Raise the expectations for transit in NYC while providing a practical vision and popular support for achieving them.

NYU | Center for Urban Science and Progress

New York University's Center for Urban Science & Progress (CUSP) is a university-wide center whose research and education programs are focused on urban informatics. Using NYC as its lab, and building from its home in the NYU Tandon School of Engineering, it integrates and applies NYU strengths in the natural, data, and social sciences to understand and improve cities throughout the world. CUSP offers a one-year MS degree in Applied Urban Science & Informatics.

TransitCenter

TransitCenter is a New York City-based foundation committed to improving public transit nationally. We do this through a combination of strategic research, advocacy, trainings, and grant-making. TransitCenter works closely with agencies and advocates alike, imparting its expertise, while pushing a strong agenda centered around high-quality transit service for riders. As an independent foundation, we can do so without bias to any political or commercial interest, which gives us great credibility and the ability to share best practice across the country.
